Instructions for the DEM Test

The Developmental Eye Movement (DEM) Test is designed to assess oculomotor function and visual processing speed. Follow these instructions carefully before beginning the test.

Before You Start:

  • Ensure you are in a quiet environment with minimal distractions.
  • Sit in a comfortable position with your screen at eye level.
  • Make sure you are well-rested and focused.

Test Procedure:

  1. Pretest: A practice session will help familiarize you with the test format.
  2. Test A: Read a series of numbers displayed vertically as fast and accurately as possible.
  3. Test B: Read another set of vertically arranged numbers.
  4. Test C: Read numbers presented in a horizontal sequence.
  5. Your time and accuracy will be recorded automatically.

Important Notes:

⚠️ Note: Avoid using a finger or cursor to guide your eyes. The goal is to assess your natural eye movements.

What Happens After the Test?

  • Your results will be displayed on the screen.
  • You can choose to retake the test if needed.
  • If advised, consult a specialist for further evaluation.
